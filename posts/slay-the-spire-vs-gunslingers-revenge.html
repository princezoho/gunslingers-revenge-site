Slay the Spire vs Gunslinger's Revenge: Which Roguelike Deckbuilder Should You Play in 2025?

When Slay the Spire launched in 2019, it created an entirely new genre: the roguelike deckbuilder. Now, Gunslinger's Revenge enters the arena with fresh innovations and a unique Wild West twist. But how do these two titans compare? This comprehensive analysis breaks down everything you need to know to choose your next obsession.

Core Gameplay Mechanics: Evolution vs Revolution

Slay the Spire's Proven Formula

Slay the Spire established the blueprint that defines roguelike deckbuilders. Players climb a branching tower, making strategic path choices between battles, shops, and events. The energy system limits card plays per turn, creating tactical decisions about resource management. With four distinct characters, each with unique mechanics and card pools, the game offers substantial variety within its established framework.

The brilliance lies in its simplicity. Draw cards, play cards using energy, defeat enemies, and acquire new cards. This elegant design makes the game immediately accessible while hiding incredible strategic depth. The relic system adds another layer, providing passive bonuses that can completely transform your strategy.

Gunslinger's Revenge's Innovation

While respecting genre conventions, Gunslinger's Revenge introduces several groundbreaking mechanics. The horse and charm system adds a customization layer beyond traditional deckbuilding, allowing players to enhance their mount with powerful abilities that complement their deck strategy. This creates an additional progression path that runs parallel to card acquisition.

The tonic crafting system brings experimentation and risk-reward decisions. Players can combine ingredients to create powerful buffs, but failed experiments might result in cursed items. This gambling element adds excitement and encourages players to take calculated risks for potentially game-changing rewards.

Most notably, the generative enemy system ensures no two encounters feel identical. Enemies are procedurally assembled from various traits, creating unique combat puzzles that require adaptive strategies rather than memorized solutions.

Character Classes and Playstyles

Slay the Spire's Focused Roster

The Ironclad embodies straightforward aggression with strength-scaling attacks and self-harm synergies. The Silent offers a more complex experience with poison stacking and shiv generation. The Defect introduces orb management, adding a puzzle element to combat. The Watcher's stance system creates a risk-reward dynamic with powerful offensive and defensive modes.

Each character feels distinct, with minimal card overlap ensuring fresh experiences. The game excels at making each class feel powerful in different ways, from the Ironclad's brutal simplicity to the Defect's calculated complexity.

Gunslinger's Revenge's Thematic Diversity

Starting with the classic Gunslinger, players unlock increasingly exotic character classes. The Bones necromancer summons undead minions and manipulates death itself. The Vampire combines life-stealing with blood magic for a high-risk, high-reward playstyle. The upcoming Mechanic class promises gadget-based gameplay with technological innovations.

Each class tells a story through its mechanics. The narrative twist—discovering you might be the villain—adds weight to character choices. This thematic integration creates a more immersive experience where gameplay and story reinforce each other.

Progression Systems: Short-term vs Long-term

Slay the Spire's Ascension Challenge

Slay the Spire's progression focuses on mastery through its Ascension system. Twenty difficulty levels introduce increasingly punishing modifiers, from stronger enemies to fewer resources. This creates a long-term goal for dedicated players while keeping runs relatively self-contained.

Card unlocks happen quickly, giving players access to full character potential within hours. This design philosophy respects player time while providing hundreds of hours of challenge through difficulty scaling rather than content gating.

Gunslinger's Revenge's RPG Elements

Gunslinger's Revenge incorporates deeper RPG mechanics with persistent character development. Players increase base stats like Strength, Charisma, and Luck across runs, creating a sense of permanent progress. The skill tree system allows for build customization that persists between attempts.

This approach provides a different satisfaction—even failed runs contribute to long-term power growth. However, it also means the game takes longer to fully experience, with some content locked behind progression walls.

Visual Design and Atmosphere

Slay the Spire's Functional Clarity

Slay the Spire prioritizes readability over visual flair. The art style is clean and functional, ensuring players can quickly parse battlefield information. Card art is distinctive but simple, focusing on mechanical clarity. This minimalist approach keeps the focus on strategic decision-making.

The gothic fantasy atmosphere is consistent but understated. Environmental storytelling is minimal, with the spire itself remaining largely mysterious. This allows players to project their own interpretations onto the world.

Gunslinger's Revenge's Stylized Western

Gunslinger's Revenge embraces its Wild West theme with hand-drawn character art inspired by spaghetti westerns. The game creates a unique atmosphere blending Western tropes with supernatural elements. Environmental details and character animations bring the world to life.

The narrative focus means more elaborate cutscenes and story moments. This creates a more cinematic experience but may interrupt flow for players seeking pure gameplay. Check out the gallery to see the distinctive art style in action.

Replayability and Content

Slay the Spire's Infinite Variety

Despite having "only" four characters, Slay the Spire offers near-infinite replayability through its combination of cards, relics, and path choices. The daily climb provides structured challenges, while custom mode allows players to create their own rule sets. The modding community has extended the game's life considerably with new characters, cards, and mechanics.

Ascension levels ensure players always have a greater challenge waiting. The game's balance is so refined that even after hundreds of hours, players discover new synergies and strategies.

Gunslinger's Revenge's Procedural Innovation

The generative enemy system is Gunslinger's Revenge's secret weapon for replayability. Rather than fighting the same enemies with predictable patterns, players face unique combinations that require adaptive thinking. The tonic crafting system's experimentation aspect means there's always something new to discover.

Multiple story paths and the narrative twist encourage replaying to see different perspectives. The game promises regular content updates with new characters, cards, and story chapters, suggesting long-term support and evolution.

Learning Curve and Accessibility

Slay the Spire's Gentle Introduction

Slay the Spire excels at teaching through play. The first character, the Ironclad, uses straightforward mechanics that introduce core concepts. The game provides just enough information without overwhelming newcomers. Tooltips explain keywords, and the UI clearly communicates all relevant information.

The base difficulty is approachable, allowing casual players to enjoy successful runs while Ascension levels provide challenge for veterans. This broad appeal has made it a streaming favorite and entry point for many into the genre.

Gunslinger's Revenge's Narrative Guidance

Gunslinger's Revenge uses its story focus to naturally introduce mechanics. Tutorial elements are woven into the narrative, making learning feel like part of the adventure rather than separate instruction. The game provides more explicit guidance early on, with characters explaining new systems as they're unlocked.

The RPG progression means players can overcome challenges through persistence, not just skill improvement. This makes the game more accessible to those who might bounce off Slay the Spire's skill-based difficulty.

Multiplayer and Social Features

Slay the Spire's Indirect Competition

While single-player only, Slay the Spire creates community through daily climbs and seed sharing. Players can compare strategies and compete for leaderboard positions. The game's clarity makes it excellent for streaming and creating educational content.

Gunslinger's Revenge's Connected Experience

Gunslinger's Revenge plans to introduce asynchronous multiplayer elements, allowing players to share custom tonics and encounter other players' ghosts in their runs. Weekly challenges with community goals create a sense of shared progress. The game is designed with streaming integration in mind, including viewer interaction features.

Platform Availability and Performance

Slay the Spire's Universal Access

Available on PC, Mac, Linux, Nintendo Switch, PlayStation, Xbox, iOS, and Android, Slay the Spire runs on virtually anything. The game's modest requirements mean it performs excellently even on older hardware. The mobile versions are particularly well-optimized with intuitive touch controls.

Gunslinger's Revenge's Modern Approach

Initially launching on PC and Mac via Steam, Gunslinger's Revenge is built with modern platforms in mind. The game is optimized for Steam Deck, recognizing the importance of portable play for the genre. Console and mobile versions are planned post-launch, with cross-progression promised across all platforms.

Community and Developer Support

Slay the Spire's Established Ecosystem

Years of community development have created extensive resources for Slay the Spire. Wikis, guides, and discussion forums provide deep strategic analysis. The developers, while moving on to new projects, left the game in a polished, complete state. The modding community continues to create new content.

Gunslinger's Revenge's Growing Frontier

As a new entry, Gunslinger's Revenge benefits from active developer engagement. Regular updates, community feedback integration, and roadmap transparency create excitement for the game's future. Price and Value Proposition

Slay the Spire's Proven Value

At its standard price point, Slay the Spire offers exceptional value with hundreds of hours of content. Frequent sales make it even more accessible. The complete experience is available immediately with no additional purchases required.

Gunslinger's Revenge's Launch Strategy

Gunslinger's Revenge plans a competitive launch price with early bird discounts for wishlist members. The developers commit to no pay-to-win elements or gameplay-affecting DLC. Future content updates will expand the story and add characters, with major additions as fairly-priced expansions.

Which Game Is Right for You?

Choose Slay the Spire If You Want:

  • A polished, complete experience available now
  • Pure strategic gameplay without narrative interruption
  • Established community and extensive resources

  • Minimalist design focused on mechanics
  • Proven long-term replayability

The Verdict: Room for Both

Rather than competing directly, Slay the Spire and Gunslinger's Revenge complement each other beautifully. Slay the Spire remains the genre's gold standard—a perfectly balanced, endlessly replayable masterpiece that every deckbuilding fan should experience. It's the foundation upon which the entire genre is built.

Gunslinger's Revenge represents the genre's evolution, taking established concepts in new directions. Its narrative focus, thematic coherence, and innovative mechanics offer experiences Slay the Spire doesn't attempt. For players who've mastered the spire, Gunslinger's Revenge provides fresh challenges and ideas.

The real winner is the player who gets to enjoy both these excellent games. Start with Slay the Spire to understand the genre's core appeal, then saddle up for Gunslinger's Revenge to see where deckbuilding is headed. The genre has room for multiple classics, and these two games prove that innovation and tradition can coexist.
